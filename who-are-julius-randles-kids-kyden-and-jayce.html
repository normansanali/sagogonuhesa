Julius Randle: High School Career

Randle went to Prestonwood Christian Academy in Plano, Texas. There, Andrew Wiggins, Jabari Parker, Andrew Harrison, and Aaron Gordon all thought of him as one of the top five players in the class of 2013.3

Randle won the Under Armour Elite 24 Dunk Contest in August 2012. The next day, he was named one of the game’s MVPs after scoring 27 points and helping his team win 164–138.

Rivals.com gave Randle five stars and said he was the best power forward and second best player in the country in 2013.

Randle broke his foot in a tournament the weekend after Thanksgiving of his senior year. He had to miss three months of basketball because of this. Randle went back to the TAPPS 5A playoffs in March 2013 and led his team to its third state championship in four years. Randle averaged 32.5 points and 22.5 rebounds per game as a senior in 2012–13.

On March 20, 2013, Randle chose Kentucky over Texas, Kansas, and Florida to be his college team. Then, he joined Andrew and Aaron Harrison, James Young, Dakari Johnson, and Marcus Lee as the sixth incoming Kentucky player chosen to play in the 2013 McDonald’s All-American Boys Game and the 2013 Jordan Brand Classic.

Julius Randle: Career

The Los Angeles Lakers picked Randle with the seventh pick in the NBA draft in 2014. Randle broke his right tibia on October 28, 2014, during the Lakers’ season opener against the Houston Rockets. He had only played in the NBA for 14 minutes at that point.

He had surgery to fix the break the next day, and it was successful. He missed the rest of the season, though. On March 9, 2015, he was given the green light to do full-weight basketball activities that did not involve contact. In July 2015, Randle was back on the court with the Lakers’ Summer League team.

Randle had 15 points and 11 rebounds in a loss to the Minnesota Timberwolves in the first game of the season. This was exactly a year after his NBA debut, when he got hurt. In a win over the Washington Wizards on December 2, he had 15 points and 19 rebounds, which was a career high at the time.

In a game against the Los Angeles Clippers on January 29, 2016, he scored 23 points, which was his career high. He tied that record on March 8, when he scored 23 points and grabbed 11 boards in a win over the Orlando Magic by a score of 107–98.

In a 116–105 loss to the Denver Nuggets on March 25, he got his first triple-double. He scored 13 points, grabbed 18 rebounds, and gave out 10 assists, which was his career high at the time.

At age 21, he became the youngest Lakers player since Magic Johnson to get a triple-double. Six days later, with 1.9 seconds left in overtime, he made a hook shot in the paint that won the game for the Lakers, 102–100, over the Miami Heat.
