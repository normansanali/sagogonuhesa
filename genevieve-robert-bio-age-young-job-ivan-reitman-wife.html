Ivan and Geneviève committed themselves to each other. They were together until Ivan died in 2022.

Sadly, Genevieve lost her filmmaker husband at the age of 75 on 12 February 2022. The family member confirmed that he died peacefully surrounded by family members at his home in Montecito, California.

The family released a joint statement that read, “Our family is grieving the unexpected loss of a husband, father, and grandfather who taught us to always seek the magic in life. We take comfort that his work as a filmmaker brought laughter and happiness to countless others around the world. While we mourn privately, we hope those who knew him through his films will remember him always.”

Ivan made a breakthrough into the movie industry with “National Lampoon’s Animal House”. He also directed comedy films such as “Meatballs” and “Stripes”. In 1984 he produced and directed the movie “Ghostbusters” which grossed $300 million. It earned two Oscar nominations, spawned a veritable franchise, including spinoffs, television shows, and a new movie, “Ghostbusters: Afterlife’, that opened this last year which his son, filmmaker Jason Reitman, directed.

Furthermore, Ivan landed a nomination for best picture Oscar alongside Jason and Daniel Dubiecki for “Up in the Air”, which Jason directed, in 2010. He was also nominated for an Emmy for best made for TV movie in 1996, as part of the producing team for “The Late Shift”, which dramatized the late-night rivalry between David Letterman and Jay Leno.
