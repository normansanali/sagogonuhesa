Daniel Masterson’s Career Highlights

Born on March 13, 1976, in Long Island, New York, Daniel Peter Masterson showed an early interest in acting and started his journey in the entertainment world at the age of eight with TV commercials.

His big break came in the early 1990s when he landed the role of Steven Hyde in the popular sitcom “That ’70s Show,” which ran from 1998 to 2006. “That ’70s Show” not only garnered him a dedicated fanbase but also provided him with steady work and a significant boost in his career.

Portraying the easygoing and funny Hyde had a positive impact on his earnings. Masterson’s acting career continued to flourish post-“That ’70s Show.” He appeared in various movies and TV series, including “Men at Work,” “Cybill,” and “Face/Off.”

One of his notable roles after the sitcom was Rooster Bennett in the Netflix series “The Ranch,” where he starred alongside Ashton Kutcher. Despite mixed reviews, this show significantly contributed to his financial success.

How Much Did ‘That 70’s Show’ Earn Danny Masterson?

Danny Masterson, known for his roles in “Beethoven’s 2nd,” “Cybill,” and as Hyde in “That ’70s Show,” had a successful acting career. Post-“That ’70s Show,” he appeared in “Punk’d,” “MADtv,” and starred alongside Jim Carrey in the 2008 movie “Yes Man.”

His collaboration with Bijou Phillips extended until the release of “The Bridge to Nowhere” in 2009. Following that, he was featured in “White Collar,” “The Chicago 8,” and played a significant role in “The Ranch” from 2016 to 2018.

During his time as Steven Hyde on “That ’70s Show,” Masterson was one of the highest-paid stars on the show, with main actors earning between $250,000 and $300,000 per episode.
