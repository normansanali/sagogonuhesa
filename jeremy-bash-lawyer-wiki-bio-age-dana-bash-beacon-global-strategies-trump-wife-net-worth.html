Jeremy Bash Career

After Jeremy graduated, he clerked for Leonie Brinkema, U.S. District Judge in the Eastern District of Virginia. He was admitted to the bars of Virginia, Maryland, the District of Columbia, the Eastern District of Virginia, and the Fourth Circuit Court of Appeals. Jeremy worked as the national security issues director for the presidential campaign of Al Gore and Joe Lieberman in 2000.

In that role, he advised the candidates, their replacement, and staff on national security policy matters, including the Middle East peace process, counter-terrorism, nonproliferation, missile defense, and trade. From 2001 -2004, he was in private law practice with the firm O’Melveny and Myers in their Washington, DC office.

His practice was based on congressional investigations, regulatory matters, and litigation. He then worked as chief minority counsel on the Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence of the U.S. House of Representatives and as an aide to California Representative Jane Haman, the committee’s top Democrat.

Jeremy was a term member of the Council on Foreign Relations. He has spoken at conferences or as part of courses for Harvard Law School, George Law School, American University, and the National War College. Jeremy was interviewed by The New York Times in regard to October 5, 2013, U.S. Special Operations Forces raid in Tripoli, Libya that resulted in the capture of Abu Anas al-Libi, a terrorist target who was indicated in the 1998 United States embassy bombings in Kenya and Tanzania.

Jeremy also featured as a commentator on PBS NewsHour and was interviewed on ABC World News regarding both the Tripoli raid and an aborted raid in Somalia to capture an al-Shabab commander known as Ikrimah.
