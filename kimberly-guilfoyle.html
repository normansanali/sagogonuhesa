Kimberly Guilfoyle is an American TV news personality who had worked at Fox News from 2006 to 2018 before which she had served as a prosecuting attorney in both San Francisco and Los Angeles. In 2018, she had started campaigning for the Republican Party in the year’s midterm elections and had later served as an advisor to Donald Trump, the then President of the United States.

Education

Kimberly had graduated from Mercy High School, an all-girls college-preparatory school in San Francisco. She had then earned a Bachelor of Arts degree from the University of California, Davis, before joining the University of San Francisco School of Law. She had graduated from there in 1994, with a Juris Doctor degree.

Kimberly had later studied at Trinity College Dublin where she had published research on international children’s rights and European Economic Community law.
