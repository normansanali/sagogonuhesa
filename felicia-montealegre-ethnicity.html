Who is Felicia Montealegre?

Felicia Montealegre Wikipedia: - Felicia Montealegre Bernstein was an actress from Costa Rica. She was born on February 6, 1922, in San José, Costa Rica. When Federico Garcia Lorca's If Five Years Pass opened in English at the Provincetown Playhouse in April 1945, Montealegre made her stage debut in New York. On July 20, 1946, at The Booth Theatre, Montealegre made her Broadway debut as the ingénue in Ben Hecht's Swan Song.

In 1976, Montealegre made her Broadway debut in the drama Poor Murderer. On May 11, 1949, Montealegre made her television debut in Mary Violet Heberden's The Oath of Hippocrates on NBC's Kraft Television Theatre as Hygieia. Montealegre was appointed as the inaugural chair of the New York Civil Liberties Union's Women's Division in 1963.
