The Reason Singer Pink Chose The Name 'Pink'

July 27, 2024
blog

Highlights

  • Pink has used her stage name since she was a teenager, inspired by a childhood nickname.
  • She came to terms with the name "Pink" after watching the character Mr. Pink from Reservoir Dogs .
  • Pink's music reflects her insecurities and struggles with societal standards, resonating with fans.

The artist known as "Pink" was born Alecia Beth Moore. But from the moment fans were introduced to her in the '90s, they've known her as Pink. So how did she come up with the name Pink (stylized as P!nk on her albums)? It's kind of a long story.

Pink has been in the industry since she was a teen. She actually started out as part of a three-person girl band but was later offered the opportunity to strike out solo.

And despite some dips and plateaus in her career, Pink continued an upward trajectory that she's still riding on today.

The singer has been open about everything from her thoughts on politics to her struggles in her marriage. Pink shares a lot about herself, and she's recently revealed even more. Including how she came up with her stage name.

Pink Was What She Went By, Even When She Was Part Of A Girl Band

Even when she was part of a teen girl group, Pink was already going by her name. So, it wasn't a record executive's decision to slap a new moniker on her.

In fact, the singer had the stage name when she first performed at about 14 years old. As multiple sources have revealed, a young Alecia was taunted with the name before she made it her own.

After releasing her album M!ssundaztood, Pink explained how she came up with the name that would become famous with her: "It's just a nickname that's been following me my whole life. It was a mean thing at first; some kids at camp pulled my pants down and I blushed so much, and they were like, 'Ha ha! Look at her! She's pink!'"

Then, the movie Reservoir Dogs came out, and the singer realized the character Mr. Pink was someone she wouldn't mind being associated with.

After that, Pink became a lot more than just her nickname. It became an identity she could (and wanted to) work with. As LiveAbout recapped, most of Pink's early albums played off her feelings of insecurity and needing to live up to society's standards for a pop star.

From "Don't Let Me Get Me," which was about not wanting to conform to being marketed like Britney Spears, to "Sober" about getting past her need to drink to be socially accepted, Pink evidently pours her emotions into her music.

Not only does her name buck trends (while also being at odds with her iconic bad-girl appearance), but her songs spoke to fans in a way that has endured over the years.

As of 2024, Pink is on tour for her latest album, Trustfall. She is touring first through Australia and the UK before she makes appearances in the US in the latter part of 2024.
