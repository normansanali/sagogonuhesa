Professional life

Eve moved to New York after high school but ended up performing as a stripper. Unsatisfied with her circumstances, she decided to pursue a music career and got a break at the age of 18. Dr. Dre signed her to Aftermath Entertainment as Eve of Destruction. However, she was let go after just a few months. Though, a song she made for the label ended up on the soundtrack of the 1998 film Bulworth. It was her first real achievement. Eve was brought to the Ruff Ryders label by Dre’s parent label, Interscope, and they signed her up and worked hard to prepare her for the big time.

Ruff Ryders’ belief in Eve was rewarded when her song “What Ya Want” reached the Billboard Top 40 in 1999. Her debut album, Let There Be Eve…, debuted at Number 1 in its first week of release in September. Eve became only the third female hip-hop artist, after Lauryn Hill and Foxy Brown, to have an album debut at No. 1. Scorpion, Eve’s second album, was released in March 2001 and sold 1.5 million copies. Its lead single, “Who’s That Girl?” rocketed her to international stardom. The second single, “Let Me Blow Ya Mind,” a Dr Dre-producedd duet with Gwen Stefani, won a Grammy in 2002. Eve-Olution, her third album, was certified gold the same year. Her collaboration with Alicia Keys, “Gangsta Lovin,'” peaked at No. 2.

Eve made her acting debut in 2002 with a role in Vin Diesel’s action-adventure film xXx. She co-starred in the hit comedy Barbershop alongside Ice Cube. Eve has starred in the film’s two sequels, released in 2004 and 2016. She appeared in the UPN comedy series Eve in 2003.
